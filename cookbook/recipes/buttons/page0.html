Enforcing minimum widths for consistency

Sometimes you don't have the same amount of content inside of a set of buttons. This isn't always a bad thing, but it can start to make your design look inconsistent, which will make you look very amateurish.

To fix this, we can apply the min-width property to enforce a minimum width for all buttons, along with the text-align property to ensure our button content stays centered.

See how even with buttons styled completely differently, they somehow look neater with the same widths? That's the power of a well-planned and consistent design!

Wide Buttons

Sometimes you'll want your buttons to completely fill the containers they're inside of.

To accomplish this, we can use a different display property value: block.

Remember, block elements take up an entire line all on their own. Since that's exactly what we want our buttons to do, it's a simple but effective way to transform our button styles! (Just remember that you'll also probably want to apply text-align, to make sure your button content is centered.)
